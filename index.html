Rules

How the pool works.

Group stage and knockout stage are separate competitions.

Each competition gets 50% of the total pot.

Group stage: guess the order, not the scorelines

For each group, rank the four teams from 1st to 4th. You are only predicting the final group order, not the match-by-match scores. Exact placements are worth 4 points, one place off is worth 2 points, two places off is worth 1 point, and a perfect group adds 4 points.

  • Exact position: 4 points
  • Off by 1 place: 2 points
  • Off by 2 places: 1 point
  • Perfect group order: +4 points

Max per group: 20 points. Tranches: Perfect = 20, Excellent = 17-19, Good = 12-16.

Group stage tiebreakers: most perfect groups, then most excellent groups, then most good groups, then most exact placements ("bullseyes").

Knockout stage: build the bracket to the champion

The knockout competition is separate from the group stage. Predict the bracket all the way from the round of 32 to the winner. Each correct advance is weighted by the odds at the time of prediction: harder, less likely calls are worth more; easy favourites are worth less. That keeps the contest skill-based without making it feel random.

  • Predict the whole bracket through to the winner
  • Odds-weighted scoring rewards bolder picks
  • Each correct advance pick is scored on its own

Knockout tiebreakers: most points in the final, then semifinal, then quarterfinal, then round of 16, then round of 32.
